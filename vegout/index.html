Our Product. Ireland's first online vegetarian take-out.

Veg Out connects lovers of vegetarian food with local restaurants in an environmentally friendly manner. Veg Out will be Ireland's first specialised vegetarian/vegan online take out service.

Veg Out will incorporate a bicycle delivery service. It will be extensively tested and regularly monitored to ensure the fastest delivery of food possible.

Product Design. mouthwateringly good.

Taking a nod from blackpepper.ie, we will design a visually pleasing, stylish and easy to navigate web interface for our customers.

We shall be using a fluid and flexible grid system, to allow each element on the screen, such as the engine for searching for dishes, to scale to fit any screen. The list of results that it generates shall also scale to fit any sized screen, as will the page with the dish info, restaurant info and map/shop location. Widths and heights of images and content divs will be set in percentages, rather than fixed width pixel sizes. Font sizes will be set in ems, rather than in pixels.

CSS3 media queries shall also be used to detect screen sizes and resolutions, and serve different CSS stylesheets accordingly. For example, using CSS3 flexboxes with a media query can adapt a column of content about the chosen record as displayed on a small mobile screen. It can be easily changed to fit into larger horizontal rows when the screen is anything wider than 320px. On rotating the phone, the content will also scale to fit nicely on both portrait and landscape views. This will be achieved also through the use of media queries.

Product Features. superiour user experience

Unlike other online restaurant aggregators such as justeat.ie, eatcity.ie and takeaway.ie, the customer will be able to search for food not only by location, but also by restaurant and dish type. If a user searches for "Pad Thai" for example, they will see a list of all of the dishes available from different restaurants. This is a unique feature enabling faster selection of dishes. Using native geolocation (via GPS/GSM/WIFI) on the customers PC or smartphone, the customer shall be able to also see a list of the take outs that are closest to their physical location.

The Veg Out service will be almost completely automated, with food payment and delivery services seamlessly integrated in one application. A Google map display for Android and IPhone will direct the delivery cyclist as to the quickest possible route from restaurant to customer destination. The app will also determine the closest available cyclist to the restaurant and assign them the order via push notifications to their smartphone. This will also be automated. Like the popular taxi app Hailo, customers will be able to see a live feed of their assigned delivery cyclist, and get live updates of the estimated time of delivery.

Reflection. implementing responsivity.

We aim to implement ADA (audience and device aware) strategies to improve the performance and usability of the site on smaller devices. Media queries again will be used to determine the screen resolution and serve images to the app according to the user's phone resolution. This will improve performance, loading times etc, whilst also serving high resolution photos to cater for hi-res devices.

CSS3 Media Queries - to detect different screen sizes and resolutions and adapt the site by serving different stylesheets accordingly. Also used to introduce new elements on the page via progressive enhancement.

CSS3 Flexboxes - to fluidly change the layout of the grid from column to row layout.

% widths and heights for divs and images - to dynamically alter the site's content to display images that fit onto the device screen.

Ems for font-size - to allow for flexible font sizes.

HTML5 - shall be used for semantic mark up, use of new localstorage and sessionstorage, audio and video elements.

jQuery and JavaScript - for interaction, user events and animations. Elements such as the logo shall be animated, as will there be swipe, tap and touchmove events incorporated to add interaction for image sliders on mobile devices

Google Maps JavaScript API - this API shall be used for our map, to find and display the user's geographical coordinates, and to display the nearest restaurant and give live updates of the dish en route.

PHP - This shall be used to display the results of the user's dish search.

MySQL - This shall be used to query the database for records; inserting, updating, deleting and selecting from it dynamically.
